Understanding Depression

Depression is a common but serious mental health condition that affects how you feel, think, and handle daily activities. It's more than just feeling sad or going through a rough patch.

Common Symptoms:

  • Persistent sadness or empty mood
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Sleep problems
  • Fatigue or low energy
  • Difficulty concentrating

Remember: Depression is treatable, and recovery is possible with the right support and treatment.

Coping Strategies

Daily Techniques:

  • Deep Breathing: Take slow, deep breaths when feeling overwhelmed
  • Grounding: Name 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear
  • Physical Activity: Even a short walk can improve mood
  • Journaling: Write down your thoughts and feelings
  • Reach Out: Connect with supportive friends or family
  • Routine: Maintain a daily schedule for stability
×

Wellness Activities

Daily Practices:

  • Morning meditation or mindfulness (5-10 minutes)
  • Gratitude journaling (3 things you're grateful for)
  • Physical exercise or movement
  • Creative activities (drawing, music, writing)
  • Nature time (even looking out a window)
  • Connecting with others
  • Limiting social media and news consumption
  • Establishing a bedtime routine
×

Support Networks

Types of Support:

  • Online Communities: Depression forums and support groups
  • Local Support Groups: In-person meetings in your area
  • Peer Support: Connect with others who understand
  • Professional Support: Therapists, counselors, psychiatrists
  • Crisis Support: 24/7 hotlines and chat services
  • Family & Friends: Building your personal support network

Remember: It's okay to need help, and seeking support is a sign of strength.
